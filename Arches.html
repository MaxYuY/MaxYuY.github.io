Arches National Park

Arches National Park is a mecca for its namesake sandstone arches, with over 2000 of them in the stunning desert landscape in eastern Utah. The park's hiking is just as good as Zion, and with plenty of amazing arches to admire, this park is certainly a top tier national park in the U.S.

There are 3 notable sections of Arches, with the first one being the Windows area, containing 4 stunning natural arches. First of which is Turret Arch (Left), which while stunning in itself, provides an awesome sunset view of the two Window Arches (center), which turn from orange to fiery red during dusk. The final arch, Double Arch (Right) can be accessible from a different trail than the other two, but it is just as cool. What's special about this arch is that as its name suggests, this arch consists of 2 separate sandstone bridges, which just looks so cool from right below. The Windows section is also the closest of the 3 to the park's entrance, giving you the best introduction to the sheer natural beauty of Arches.

The Delicate Arch area consists of just one hike, but is certainly the best one in Arches. The hike is only a few miles round-trip, and is 100% worth it, especially in sunset where the arch turns to gorgeous red. With the gorgeous desert scenery all around, Delicate Arch certainly earns its title as one of the most iconic attractions of any national park.

While Delicate Arch is the single most iconic arch in the park, the Devils Garden area is home to the largest collection of these cool sandstone formations. This includes Landscape arch (Top left), one of the longest natural arches in the entire world, Double O Arch (Top center), with 2 sandstone bridges creating two O shaped openings, and Partition arch (Top right), whose higher elevation yields plenty of gorgeous desert views of the Devil's Garden. Aside from the natural arches, the scenery of Devils Garden hike itself is just as stunning, especially paired with the snowy mountains in the backdrop. I also got to see plenty of chipmunks on the trail and even a few Mule Deer (Bottom Right) at the trailhead, which was really cool. Ultimately, all three main sections of this park are equally unique and certainly make Arches one of the most revered natural wonders in the entire United States.
