Kauai Reefs

While not as much as Maui, The reefs and beaches of Kauai are still so biodiverse, with tons of cool coral reefs and marine wildlife to be found, both on the beach and underwater, making the island paradise really cool in my opinion.

Places such as Koloa Landing (left), Poipu Beach (center, right), and Lawai Beach are some of my favorite snorkeling spots on Kauai, featuring a really cool array of marine life and coral reefs that I was really impressed with.

Such marine life included tons of beautiful Sea turtles (top left, top center, bottom left, bottom center), by far my favorite marine animal to swim with and one of the coolest creatures found on this planet, as well as a striking Eagle Ray, which seemed like a juvenile ray due to its small size. Both the Ray and the large amount of sea turtles I found at Koloa Landing Bay, making the place my favorite snorkeling spot in Kauai and one of my favorite in Hawaii.

At Poipu Beach, I got to see a ton of marine life on the beach itself. This included the famous daily turtle arrival (top left) on the beach in the evening, so special to witness every night here. These turtles make their way back to sea at dawn, which I also got to catch one beautiful turtle (top center, top right) leaving the beach pretty late into the morning. Besides turtles, Poipu beach is the common spot to see rare Hawaiian Monk Seals (bottom row), which I found a few resting on the beach and on the island across from Poipu, both really cool and adorable to witness in person. Overall, Kauai's beaches and reefs and the variety of marine life found there are so cool, making the island itself a top tier destination to travel to.
